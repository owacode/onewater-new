How we started

In 2012, a group of people led by Archana Suresh came together to collect old but reusable items (clothes, toys, footwear, utensils etc.) in an apartment community in Hyderabad, India. The only thing they had in common - unbridled passion to help the underprivileged. Up until 2020, this group of people that called itself Bring A Smile Foundation, has reached many milestones. Notable among them, distribution of more than 50 tons of basic necessities in 7 cities and 4 rural areas of India.

From education to infrastructure assistance, Bring A Smile Foundation has been relentless in it's pursuit to improve lives.

Educating over 250 school children from economically backward strata, building classrooms / orphanage infrastructure in 4 government schools and 3 destitute homes, and partnering with various collaborators working across the spectrum of the UN's Sustainable Development Goals has seen this registered charitable Trust take the form of a movement.

The pandemic has seen Bring A Smile Foundation double down in its effort to alleviate suffering of people especially by distribution of food, sanitary pads and masks.

There is also the additional objective to bridge the Digital Divide for underprivileged children by working closely with Information Technology companies (for refurbished laptops) and various Government departments, to make online education accessible to all.
